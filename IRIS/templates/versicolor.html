Iris Versicolor

Iris versicolor, commonly known as the blue flag, harlequin blueflag, larger blue flag, northern blue flag, and poison flag, goes by various names. In Britain and Ireland, it is referred to as the purple iris. Here are some key details about this beautiful flowering plant:

Color: The attractive flowers range from deep blue to purple, and rarely white.

Habitat: It thrives in marshes, meadows, fields, and along the shores of rivers or lakes.

Medicinal Use: Native Americans used it externally for burns, wounds, swellings, and sores, and internally for liver and kidney disease
