Iris setosa

Iris setosa, also known as the bristle-pointed iris, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Iris. Here are some key details about it:

Appearance: Iris setosa has tall, branching stems with mid-green, grass-like leaves. The leaves are lanceolate (sword-shaped) and can measure 30 to 60 cm (12 to 24 in) long by 0.8 to 2.5 cm wide. The large flowers, which bloom between June and July, are 5to8 cm (3to6 in) across and come in shades of blue, violet, purple-blue, and lavender. Occasionally, there are pink or white forms.

Rhizomes: The shallowly rooted, large, branching rhizomes spread over time to create large clumps.
